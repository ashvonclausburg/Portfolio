Case Study · Product Design

Invoices
Null State Redesign

Using insights and instincts, increased the invoices null state CTR by ~20%

Company Square
Role Product Designer
Focus UX, Conversion

Background

The null state wasn't working.

The Invoices funnel had a 55% drop-off rate from sellers landing on the product page to sending their first invoice. Rather than offering guidance or context, the dashboard simply showed them empty data, leaving new sellers with no clear path forward.

Export: null-state-old-funnel.png (screenshot of old Invoices overview page)

Research

What we knew, and what we didn't.

We had a hypothesis: if we gave sellers more context on the Invoices Overview page, covering the product, its features, and the benefits, so more of them would send their first invoice. But the page needed to work for two very different users.

What we knew

Two distinct audiences

Sellers who signed up specifically for Invoices needed a clearer CTA. Sellers casually exploring Square needed context on why Invoices would help their business. Both groups would benefit from understanding features and functionality.

What we didn't know

Why sellers were dropping off

We had limited directional data on why sellers were abandoning the funnel before sending their first invoice, or what kind of content would actually move them to act.

Competitive Audit

Understanding the landscape.

We audited null states across five competitors and several internal Square product pages. Three repeatable patterns emerged, each with a distinct tradeoff between user control and information density.

Exploration

Three approaches.

Approach 01

Pop-up overlay

An informational guide overlays the product page to walk users through key features, without changing the underlying layout.

Pros
  • Perfectly sequences information without layout changes
  • Dismissible, so experts can skip immediately
Cons
  • High dismiss risk and hard to find again later
  • People hate pop-ups

Honeybook

Approach 02

Temporary setup page

A dedicated setup page surfaces in the nav and guides users through key actions. Once setup is complete, the page disappears.

Pros
  • Persistent during setup without disrupting the main product
  • Users can return to it as needed
Cons
  • Easy to skip entirely during onboarding
  • Disappears before some users are ready

Approach 03

Dynamic null state

The product page itself adapts, surfacing CTAs, educational content, and feature context that evolves as the user engages. Once used, the page shifts toward the standard product view.

Pros
  • Speaks to both new and nearly-active users
  • Lets marketing, education, and product content coexist
Cons
  • More complex to design and test the evolution logic
  • Can feel disorienting if transitions are not handled well

Process

Cross-functional brainstorm.

With the evolving page direction decided, we ran a cross-functional session with engineering, product, marketing, and design to align on content strategy. Three questions framed the conversation:

01

How do we get sellers started?

What first step (or sequence of steps) should we encourage Invoice-ready sellers to take?

02

What should sellers know?

Which product features are most worth calling out to sellers who are just exploring Invoices?

03

Why should they use it?

What business benefits would resonate most with sellers who haven't yet committed to using Invoices?

A/B Testing

Two bets, one experiment.

Given how different our two target audiences were, we decided to A/B test two distinct approaches rather than try to serve both with a single page. One marketing-led page emphasizing high-level product benefits, and one set-up-led page focused on guiding sellers through their first steps.

Hypothesis

Sellers shown a more contextual, action-oriented null state will be more likely to send their first invoice than sellers shown the current empty dashboard.

Final Design

How the page evolves.

The dynamic null state adapts as sellers engage. The page opens with a marketing-forward layout built for new users, shifts to an action-oriented view after the first send, then settles into the standard product experience once the seller is active.
